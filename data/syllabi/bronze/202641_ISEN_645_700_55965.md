## Catalog Description

Lean Engineering. (3-0). Credit 3. Introduces the principles of lean thinking in modern manufacturing systems; philosophical, managerial and organizational requirements studied; lean manufacturing quantitative modeling methodologies, lean manufacturing cell design and case study analysis. Prerequisites: ISEN 609 or approval of instructor.

## Additional Course Details

I will provide a more detailed description of the material for each week/class in the lecture notes.

Lean engineering is a blend of principle and practice applied to production systems. A lean production system is one that generates goods and services with minimal buffering of products and resources. Systems engineering provides a compendium of methods and models to represent and characterize the complex nature of production systems and their interdependencies. The course is architected to expose the students to the knowledge, skills, and experiences (KSEs) of lean engineering applied to production systems. The semester project provides the experiential component, the text and state of the practice readings provide the knowledge, and classroom exercises, management tools, and homework provide the backbone for the skills dimension.

The core principles of lean production systems are summarized as value, value stream, flow, control, and perfection . The lectures and class discussions have been organized around these 5-core principles. Like most systems, the LPS requires maintenance, or it decays. The principle of perfection alludes to the never-ending drive towards the 'lean production system upon a hill' - that is, true single-piece flow and quantity control enabled through quality control. But it is during LPS design that we must instrument for waste accounting and PS 'health' monitoring. 6σ (variability reduction) is intrinsic to LPS design.

Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:

# 4. Architect, using SE methods, a productive organization that is congruent with a lean-6σ culture.

## Grading Policy
The content of this course material must be internalized by you to be of ultimate value in your own professional career. The approach that I will use to help you internalize the knowledge of Lean systems is simple: Recitation + Application + Enriched Repetition. I will use the quizzes as a way to ensure you have initiated this learning process and we will then use lean exercise and project work to help you fully internalize the concepts of Lean Thinking and Lean Manufacturing.
Quizzes [15%] - A quiz will be given weekly; a time window will be identified for initiating the quiz along with a time available for its completion once initiated.
Homework [15%] - there will be several small, design, modeling, and analysis exercises assigned throughout the semester. These exercises will be used to drive home key principles and practices of Lean. Each HW assignment will make clear whether it is individual or team based. In the later half of the semester the HW assignments will be coordinated with elements of your project to serve as draft artifacts in your project design.
Midterm exam [35%] - this take-home exam will cover basic principles, practices, and concepts from systems engineering, factory physics, and material flow.
Project Work [35%] - Additionally I will assign a team-based project during the semester that will pull together concepts from the lean homework but will be focused on an entire production system design and/or operation. A rubric for the project will be provided when we initiate the project.
Final grades will be assigned as follows: A: [90,100] B: [80-90) C: [70,80) D: [60,70) F: [0,60)

## AI Statement
### Leveraging AI technology as a resource
The ability to rapidly assimilate information across a large number of disparate sources is very useful to the engineer. Unfortunately, the use and acceptance of AI technology is still in its infancy and the veracity of the AI generated information is not easily vetted nor is there a standard process by which the vetting is acceptable across the academic community. Regardless, it is a resource - but due to its volatility (same prompt, different AI-bot, varied response) and sometimes questionable provenance (what, exactly, did it assimilate in order to generate its response) the use of this technology must be carefully documented by you. The burden of veracity is placed on the user.
If you choose to leverage AI technologies of any type then you must, append to your submission, the following documentation:
    # 5. A full verification, by you, of the response from the AI-bot; this is akin to a literature review for each of the points made by the AI-bot that were leveraged by you in your submission
    ## 6. A statement, signed by you, attesting to the veracity of the response from the AI-bot
    Clearly, with a timed quiz, use of AI technology makes little sense - since you would have the added burden of supplying the aforementioned documentation as part of the timed submission. Additionally, the HW, midterm, and project are largely modelbased, thus the use of AI may also be of limited value versus the additional documentation required.
    If you have questions regarding the use of AI, please ask.
