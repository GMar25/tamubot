## Additional Course Details


This course studies first-order optimization methods from a perspective that blends geometry, operator theory, and algorithmic design. Topics covered include centralized and decentralized gradient descent, mirror descent, their stochastic variants, accelerated methods, together with their convergence theory.


## Course Prerequisites


Prerequisite/Corequisite(s): Approval of instructor.

Familiarity with proof-based undergraduate-level linear algebra and real analysis is needed. Experience with a graduate-level optimization course, such as ISEN 622/623 and MATH 651/652, is recommended but not required.


## Course Learning Outcomes


Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:

- Prove convergence rates for first-order methods

- Select the appropriate algorithm for a given problem and justify the choice

- Implement and evaluate deterministic and stochastic first-order methods

- Compare first order methods in terms of performance

- Derive and analyze decentralized first order methods

- Explain the relationship between centralized, decentralized and federated optimization methods

- Read and critically evaluate modern research papers on first-order and distributed optimization
