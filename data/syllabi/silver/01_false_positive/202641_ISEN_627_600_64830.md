## Catalog Description

Engineering Analysis for Decision Making. (3-0). Credit 3. Principles and application of techniques in analysis of decision processes involving engineering systems under uncertainty; areas of utility and information theory as related to quantification of information for decision-making. Prerequisites: ISEN 609; STAT 601 or approval of instructor.

## Course Prerequisites

Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):

ISEN 609; STAT 601 or approval of instructor.

## Course Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:

- Apply Bayesian approaches to perception and decisions under uncertainty.

- Implement computational models for Bayesian inference and strategic decision support.

- Use concepts from economics, psychology, and management science to analyze decision-making processes.

## Course Schedule

Week 1: Introduction, Probability / Bayes's Theorem

Week 2: Distributions / Estimating Proportions

Week 3: Estimating Counts / Odds and Addends

Week 4: Minimum, Maximum, and Mixture / Poisson Processes

Week 5: Decision Analysis / Testing

Week 6: Exam #1 / Comparison

Week 7: Classification / Inference

Week 8: Survival Analysis / Mark and Recapture

Week 9: Linear Regression

Week 10: Logistic Regression

Week 11: Exam #2 / Project Introduction and Coordination

Week 12: Conjugate Priors / MCMC

Week 13: Approximate Bayesian Computation / Work on Project

Week 14: Project Intermediate Review / Thanksgiving Break
