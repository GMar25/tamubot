## Catalog Description


Production and Inventory Control. (3-0). Credit 3. Model development for inventory management and for production planning; production control models for line balancing, lot sizing, dispatching, scheduling, releasing, kitting, MRP and just-in-time with treatment of flexible manufacturing and assembly. Prerequisites: ISEN 620; ISEN 609.




## Course Prerequisites


Prerequisite/Corequisite(s): ISEN 620; ISEN 609.


## Course Learning Outcomes


Provide an in-depth coverage of the analytical methods used to solve operations management problems and support the production function. Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:

- describe different types and levels of decision making in a production system

- model, formulate, solve, analyze problems arising in aggregate production and supply chain planning

- model and analyze fundamental inventory control systems with certain/uncertain demand

- describe and analyze push (MRP) and pull production systems (JIT)

- model and solve deterministic operations scheduling problems

- model and solve basic project scheduling problems
