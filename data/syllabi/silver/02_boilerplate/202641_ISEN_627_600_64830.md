## Catalog Description


Engineering Analysis for Decision Making. (3-0). Credit 3. Principles and application of techniques in analysis of decision processes involving engineering systems under uncertainty; areas of utility and information theory as related to quantification of information for decision-making. Prerequisites: ISEN 609; STAT 601 or approval of instructor.


## Course Prerequisites


Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):

ISEN 609; STAT 601 or approval of instructor.


## Course Learning Outcomes


Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:

- Apply Bayesian approaches to perception and decisions under uncertainty.

- Implement computational models for Bayesian inference and strategic decision support.

- Use concepts from economics, psychology, and management science to analyze decision-making processes.
